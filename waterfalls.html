Dudhsagar Falls ( lit. 'Sea of Milk') is a four-tiered waterfall on the Mandovi River in the Indian state of Goa. It is 60 km from Panaji by road and is located on the Belgaum-Vasco Da Gama rail route about 46 km east of Madgaon and 80 km south of Belgaum.
Dudhsagar Waterfalls In Goa HomeIndia TourismGoa Tourismplaces to visit in GoaDudhsagar Waterfalls Dudhsagar Waterfalls Nestled between Goa and Karnataka border is the majestic Dudhsagar Waterfalls, which when literally translated means sea of milk in Konkan. Located in Sanguem Taluka, near Bhagwan Mahaveer Wildlife Sanctuary, Mollem, the waterfall originates from Karnataka flowing from the Deccan Plateau into the river Mandovi towards the imposing Sahyadri Western Ghats cascading from a height of 300 meters and splitting into three streams, forming a deep green pool with clouds of milky foam rising up at the base before continuing its journey to join the Arabian Sea in the west. The magnificent waterfall is an enchanting spectacle to behold as it vehemently gushes down appearing as a heavy stream of milk flowing effortlessly from a distance. Perched at the top of a crescent shaped valley and surrounded by lush pristine verdant forest with a rich biodiversity, Dudhsagar Waterfalls offers an unparalleled, magical and mesmerizing view that attracts a large number of local and foreign tourists.

2.KUNTHALA WATERFALLS

KUNTHALA WATERFALLS

These waterfalls are in the dense forests inhabited by the Gonds. Kunta in Gondi and Telugu means pond. Kuntalu means several ponds. The waterfall originates from a confluence of several ponds that lead to the river Kadem.[3] Formed by the Kadam River, Kuntala falls cascades down through two steps and can be seen as two separate adjacent falls after the peak rains. It is one of the famous one day outings from Hyderabad. There is a motorable road until the entry point of the falls, from where steps are available to reach the bottom of the falls. The falls are about 10 minutes (one way) walk from the entry point. Kuntala Waterfall, nestled in the Sahyadri mountain range in Adilabad district, Telangana, India, is the state's highest waterfall with a height of approximately 200 feet. Situated on Kadem River, a tributary of the Godavari, it cascades gracefully in a region replete with breathtaking natural beauty. This enchanting waterfall is surrounded by dense forests that are part of the Kuntala Khurd village, providing a picturesque backdrop for nature enthusiasts and photographers alike. The descent to the falls requires trekkers to navigate through a well-marked but steep pathway that adds a sense of adventure to the visit. The sound of the gushing waters complements the serenity of the lush greenery, making Kuntala Waterfall a rejuvenating getaway. The falls are particularly magnificent during the monsoon season when the water is at its fullest. The abode of natural charm attracts large numbers of tourists seeking a tranquil retreat from the hustle of urban life.
